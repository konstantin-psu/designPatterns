Overall

  • What is the best feature of this course?
    • Instructor.
    • All the patterns we learned about.
    • Useful.
    • The knowledge gained is very useful.
    • Homework.
    • Question & Answers and HW.
    • Learning Pattens.
    • Learning the new patterns concepts, they were distilled into slides very well.
    • Subject matter, plus in Java. Also liked: Quick assignment feedback; On-line solutions when we got stuck, or just to see how you did it.
    • Lots of code.
    • The course has too many patterns & too many assignments. I expect the Prof. to teach only important patterns & then devote half the time on teaching Java in class which will use for the assignments. E.g., some of the homework were done in Swing, so I expect Prof to cover that part in class. In short, it would be a great course if Prof. mixes this ``DPJ'' with his previous ``Advanced Course in Java''.
    • Practical application of the course.
    • Right mix of theory and practice.
    • Assignments.
    • All in all a great course!
    • Interaction in class.
    • Context.
    • A new subject.
    • Solid OO Design concepts.

    • Some weeks prog. assign. got up to 7.

    • Homework.

    • Teach advanced features of Java.

  • What would you remove from this course?
    • A better text.
    • Study less number of patterns.
    • I'd eliminate GUI-related code or provide it as part of the exercise. I have taken the Java course over a year ago and forgot much of the GUI related API. At times it was difficult to see the ``forest for the trees'' meaning I was coding the solution too much and not focusing on the pattern I was implementing.
    • Some of the trivial patterns, e.g., some of the earlier patterns covered during the first half of the course.
    • Keep it the same.
    • It will be good as it is.
