Design Pattern Workshop

When developing software, you encounter a number of recurring problems. A pattern is a reusable solution to a recurring problem. Mark Grand, noted consult and book author is offering a three day workshop on patterns for object-oriented design. All of the design examples will use the UML notation. All of the code examples will use Java.

Workshop Format

The workshop will consist of discussions of patterns followed by design and coding exercises that will incorporate the patterns that were just discussed.

Who Should Attend?

Software architects and senior programmers will develop a common vocabulary for discussing design concepts. They will less frequently find themselves spending an hour debating the merits of an idea with someone, only to find that they had both had the same idea but were using different words.  In addition to learning new design concepts, they will develop a better understanding of when to use design concepts they may already be familiar with

Junior and mid-level programmers who are familiar with the basics of object-oriented design will learn design concepts that it would take them years to discover on their own. They will also be able to better understand and implement designs that are given to them.
